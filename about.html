Our Story

From humble beginnings to becoming Africa's most trusted media network

Founded in 2010, Born from a Vision

CrownRoute Africa Network began as a small digital publication with a bold vision: to create a pan-African media house that would tell Africa's stories from an African perspective. Our founders, veteran journalists from across the continent, saw the need for a media platform that would go beyond stereotypes and showcase Africa's complexity, diversity, and dynamism.

What started as a team of five journalists working from a shared office in Nairobi has grown into a network of over 300 dedicated professionals across 25 African countries. Today, we reach millions of readers daily through our digital platforms, television partnerships, and radio broadcasts.

Our growth has been fueled by a commitment to journalistic integrity, innovative storytelling, and deep investment in African talent. We've pioneered new forms of digital journalism on the continent while maintaining the highest standards of factual reporting.

Our Mission

To deliver accurate, impactful, and comprehensive journalism that informs, empowers, and connects Africans across the continent and diaspora. We strive to be the most trusted source of African news and analysis, fostering informed citizenship and democratic engagement.

Our Vision

To become Africa's definitive media network, shaping the continent's narrative for global audiences while serving as the primary source of news and information for Africans everywhere. We envision a media ecosystem where African stories are told with depth, nuance, and respect.

Our Core Values

The principles that guide our journalism and operations

Integrity

We maintain the highest ethical standards in our reporting, ensuring accuracy, fairness, and transparency in all our journalism. Truth is non-negotiable.

Pan-Africanism

We believe in Africa's unity and diversity. Our coverage transcends borders, highlighting both shared challenges and unique successes across the continent.

Amplifying Voices

We prioritize marginalized voices and ensure that women, youth, rural communities, and minority groups are heard in our coverage of African affairs.

Innovation

We embrace new technologies and storytelling methods to make African journalism more accessible, engaging, and impactful for digital audiences.

Collaboration

We partner with local journalists, media organizations, and civic groups across Africa to deepen our coverage and support media development.

Accountability

We hold power to account while being accountable to our audience. We correct errors promptly and explain our editorial decisions transparently.

Our Continental Reach

Our Continental Presence

  • Headquarters: Nairobi, Kenya - Our central hub for East African coverage
  • West Africa Bureau: Lagos, Nigeria - Covering Africa's largest economy and most populous nation
  • Southern Africa Bureau: Johannesburg, South Africa - Focusing on business, mining, and regional politics

  • Francophone Africa Bureau: Dakar, Senegal - Serving French-speaking African nations
